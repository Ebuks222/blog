**CP Giwa's Border Security Offensive Records Fresh Breakthrough as Enugu Police Arrest Armed Suspect**





**The Enugu State Police Command has continued to strengthen border security under the Nigeria Police Force's Handshake Patrol initiative, recording another breakthrough with the arrest of a 25-year-old suspect for unlawful possession of a firearm.**

**The suspect, Ogbonna Nnaji, was apprehended by operatives of the Command's Distress Response Squad (DRS) during a stop-and-search operation at Ugwuoba in Oji River Local Government Area, along the Enugu–Onitsha Expressway near the Enugu–Anambra border. A search conducted on him led to the recovery of a concealed locally made double-barrel pistol.**

**Preliminary investigations revealed that the suspect boarded a commercial Toyota Sienna vehicle from Solution Park in Ekwulobia, Aguata Local Government Area of Anambra State, heading to Enugu before he was intercepted by the police.**

**The Command said investigations are ongoing to determine the source of the firearm and establish the suspect's possible involvement in other criminal activities. He will be charged in court upon the conclusion of investigations.**

**The arrest comes just four days after the same DRS operatives recovered another firearm from a suspect travelling in a tricycle during a similar border security operation, underscoring the Command's sustained efforts to prevent the influx of illegal weapons into Enugu State.**

**Since assuming office, Commissioner of Police Mamman Bitrus Giwa has intensified intelligence-led policing, border surveillance, and proactive crime-fighting operations across Enugu State. The Command has recorded several operational successes in its ongoing efforts to combat violent crime, cultism, and the proliferation of illegal firearms.**

**CP Giwa commended the operatives for their vigilance and professionalism and reaffirmed the Command's commitment to sustaining robust border security operations. He also urged residents to remain security conscious and continue providing timely and credible information to support the police in maintaining peace and security across the state.**
